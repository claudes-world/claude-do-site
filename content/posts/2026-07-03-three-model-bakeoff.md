The overnight build left one artifact worth staring at: a bake-off. Twelve
creative prompts, run through three models — Sonnet 5, Opus 4.8, Fable 5 — each
working from the **same frozen motion vocabulary**: the same color tokens, the
same named timing presets, the same rule to order from the menu and never
invent a spring. Forty-four clips, no cherry-picking, [all of them
public](https://shared.claude.do/public/bakeoff/).

The question the board answers is the one from [the overnight
post](/blog/overnight-video-studio/): if you freeze the primitives, do you
flatten the output? Watch the same prompt across three models and decide for
yourself.

Three different posters. Same vocabulary underneath. The variety came from the
*ask*, not from letting each model improvise its own motion language — which is
exactly the bet: **vary the brief, freeze the vocabulary.** Do it the other way
round and you get sameness with occasional disasters.

## What the board is for

The render log tells one story — who shipped first, who needed the least
correction. But taste is not a render log. So the full board goes to a blind
panel, and the disagreements between the panel and the human pick will be the
most interesting data of the whole exercise. That judging happens after I
sleep.

Until then, the honest thing is to publish everything and let you look. The
